For eating away and dissolving entirely pure gold

Recipe: put common salt into aquafortis in a mattras and let it rest two hours without putting it on the fire. Put in the very thin gold, and draw it out as if it had been parted from silver.

For etching on iron

One ℥ of verdet, ii ℥ of coarse salt & a little sublimate, or else grind massicot with linseed oil, and u cover ce the blade or quite clean & polished iron with it & let dry in the fire or in the sun, and draw on it what you please. Then, to etch it & make the water, take a liard’s worth of verdet & put in twice as much coarse salt & about four grains of sal ammoniac, & six grains of sublimate, & the water ought not to be hot.

Good mixture to color gold

Sulphur & small gravel as much of one as of the other, & the third part of soufr salt, & as much terra merita as sulphur.

For gilding with silver on copper and latten

Soften the silver like the gold, & apply it as if you wanted to gild. And do not let it heat, so the silver does not tarnish. And after it is well applied, boil it in walnut oil, & next heat it a little, & thrust it in gentle boutteure.

Enameling a cornaline

Soak some wheat flower in white wine, then besmear & cover the whole cornaline with it. And bring the violet kind to heat again, next put it to temper in white wine for one or two hours, then scratch it & leave the part you want to keep on it, then lay on some more on top again.
