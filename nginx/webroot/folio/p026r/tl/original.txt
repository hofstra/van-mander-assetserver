<head>Petard</head>

<ab>They are made better in crucible form, <del>hollow at the</del>
narrower at the bottom &amp; becoming larger towards the muzzle. It is
necessary to pestle the <m>powder</m> thoroughly &amp; put on top of it either
sand or <m>cork</m> or better still a fitted plate of <m>wax</m>
thoroughly <tl>pestled</tl> on top, for it seals precisely &amp; makes for
greater force. The ball only makes a hole, the <m>powder</m> alone makes
more of an effect. One holds that, once loaded &amp; kept in a dry place
for fifteen days, they have more force.</ab>
