Oil

All oil which is imbibed by paper & once heated by fire evaporates & leaves the paper clean, like turpentine & spike lavender oil, is good for making varnish.

Sand

Everything ground & tempered becomes porous, according to some.

Bellows furnace

Before you put in the metal, it is necessary that it be red at the bottom, like like a charcoal & well inflamed. Next, you fill it & cover the charcoal & adjust the bellows, otherwise the metal on top would melt by means of the bellows & its bottom would be curdled & cooled & would not run. But if you proceed in this manner you will melt everything you like.

Varnish on paper

The Germans make boxes p covered with painted paper & varnish it with glair of egg mixed with gum & a little oil, not of spike lavender, but another odorant that resembles olive oil. Every work done with glair supports oil. It is with this that painters trick the poor peasants, painting their bands of taffeta with this glair, to be done sooner. But the first rain carries away everything.
