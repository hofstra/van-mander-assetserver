Hard wax for imprinting seals

It is made from white wax, which is harder than the other, & one mixes in very finely ground ceruse or lead white until it becomes as hard as you fancy u, adding to it a drop of turpentine to bind it. Next, mix in whatever color you want. That is the wax goldsmiths use for modeling.

Feet of smalls lizards for gold and silver

Because the feet of these, which are very small, are very delicate, as you molded the top and d uncovered the belly & the bottom of the feet, cover them lightly with wax & then cast the second mold. And the wax being removed, it leaves a g little gate for the aforesaid feet. And should the bottom of these come of in one piece, you can repair it. And the scales from the top of the toes will come out well.

Clamps and broken mold

When you have made the first reheating & removed the clamps, and you have the opened your mold to clean it by blowing, with quicksilver, which is used only in enclosed molds, close the mold again & put the clamps on again, but not in the first place where they were. Lute again & dry again. But if it is for a cast with silver or gold, lute with the remainder of the sand that was used, for it is the safest. If your mold, while reheating, is broken, you can adjust it with some clamps & lute.
