To dry preserves take a which are not liquid, take an open tub pierced on both sides with a small, square window, & pass sticks through these on one side and the other, & place on these a sieve made of strings in the form of a net. And on this, you will readily place the vessels with your preserves. And having put at the bottom of the tub a chafing-dish or two, cover the tub with a sheet folded double.

For making a horse to follow

+One needs to give it sweet bread & it will know the one who will make him this goody.
And for maintaining in a good disposition for walking along, put it, make it take a good gait from the start of the walk & continue it.

Dogs

For keeping them from biting, it is good to have little resin balls formed like those of shoemakers & to throw to them as they yap at you, & they will bite on the balls so much that their teeth will be stuck together & they will not be able to hurt you.

For disguising a horse

Cut its ears & tail, & with lit hay, singe the hair on its head & elsewhere.

Boots of hay

For lack of boots, you can make rope of hay & wrap your legs in it, & the rain will never pass through.
